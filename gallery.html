Poon Hill

Mustang

Poon Hill is a famous viewpoint located at an elevation of 3,210 meters (10,531 feet) in the Annapurna region of Nepal. It is primarily known for its stunning panoramic views of the sunrise over the Himalayan ranges, including Dhaulagiri I (the world's 7th highest mountain), Annapurna I (10th highest), Machhapuchhre (Fishtail), Nilgiri, and Hiunchuli peaks. .

Mustang refers to a remote, culturally rich, high-altitude region in the Himalayas of northern Nepal, once an independent kingdom, known as the "Forbidden Kingdom" for its isolation, preserving Tibetan Buddhist culture, ancient monasteries, stark desert landscapes, and unique traditions like sky burials, accessible via trekking permits for adventurous travelers. It's a trans-Himalayan land of stunning beauty, dramatic gorges, and a preserved Tibetan way of life, featuring places like the walled city of Lo Manthang and sacred sites like Muktinath.
